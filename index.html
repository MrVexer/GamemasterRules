Vengeful Realms

Story Team Code and Conduct

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish expectations, responsibilities, and standards for members of the Story Team.

Story Team members are responsible for facilitating engaging roleplay, creating story opportunities, maintaining narrative consistency, and supporting player-driven storytelling throughout Vengeful Realms.

The Story Team exists to serve the roleplay environment, not to control it.

1. Story Team Conduct

1.1 Professionalism

Story Team members must remain impartial at all times.

Personal friendships, prior faction affiliations, character relationships, or player reputation must never influence event outcomes or narrative decisions.

Story Team members are expected to treat all players respectfully and professionally.

Disagreements should be handled calmly and constructively. If common ground cannot be found, escalate it to a senior member of the team.

Story Team members should encourage participation rather than gatekeep it.

1.2 Confidentiality

Future story plans, event details, staff discussions, and internal narrative documents are confidential.

Information learned through Story Team duties must never be used on personal characters.

Players should never receive advance information regarding planned events, investigations, or narrative developments unless this is absolutely required for them to play a specific role within it.

1.3 Personal Characters

Members of the Story Team may maintain personal characters subject to current staff policy:

  • Must not hold a permanent position within any hostile roleplay faction such as Legion, Thalmor, or Stormcloaks.
  • Must not hold a permanent position of authority such as Jarl, Hold Court, Law Enforcement, or Faction Leadership.

Personal characters must never receive preferential treatment.

Story Team members may not use Story Team knowledge to benefit their own characters, factions, businesses, or allies.

Story Team members may be requested to temporarily step away from personal roleplay, or restrict their access to Story Team channels, where conflicts of interest arise.

2. Philosophy of Storytelling

2.1 Player Agency First

The role of the Story Team is to create opportunities, not predetermined outcomes.

Players should be allowed to influence events through meaningful roleplay and decision-making.

Events should present challenges and consequences, but should not railroad participants into specific outcomes. Exceptions to this do exist.

The most memorable stories are those created collaboratively between players and Story Team members.

2.2 Facilitation Over Control

Story Team members should avoid becoming the focus of events.

The purpose of an event is for players to engage with the story, not to watch Story Team characters perform it.

Where possible:

  • Players should solve problems.
  • Players should uncover mysteries.
  • Players should make important decisions.
  • Players should determine outcomes.

Story Team should provide the framework, not the spotlight. It is okay to offer a nudge if players appear to be stuck or unable to progress.

2.3 Failure Is A Valid Outcome

Not every event must end in success.

Players should be allowed to fail if their actions, preparation, or decisions lead to failure.

Failure should create new roleplay opportunities rather than simply ending a storyline.

3. Event Creation

3.1 Narrative Standards

Events should:

  • Fit the setting and established lore.
  • Be accessible to a reasonable number of players.
  • Encourage roleplay over combat whenever possible.
  • Have meaningful consequences.
  • Create opportunities for future stories.

Most events should avoid relying on a single player, faction, or outcome.

Typically, any events beyond basic interactions or job-quests should be reasonably written out and discussed with a senior gamemaster.

Senior gamemasters should also discuss these events with one another to ensure the quality is high and potential consequences are noted.

3.2 Risk & Consequences

High-risk events should have clear stakes.

Potential outcomes should be considered before an event begins.

Consequences should be proportional to player actions and decisions.

Major developments including:

  • Major political changes
  • Large-scale wars
  • Faction destruction
  • Significant lore changes
  • Server-wide crises
  • Permanent character death

Require approval from the Story Team's Head of Staff.

3.3 Fairness

All factions and player groups should have equal opportunity to engage with server storylines.

Story Team members must not create events solely for the benefit of their friends, preferred factions, or personal roleplay interests.

Factions who have not had access to recent events may open tickets to request GM assistance and should have slight priority.

4. Story Characters

4.1 Narrative Characters

Story Team members may portray temporary narrative characters including:

  • Nobles
  • Merchants
  • Criminals
  • Cultists
  • Soldiers
  • Travellers
  • Quest givers
  • Historical figures

These characters exist to facilitate stories.

They are not permanent protagonists or antagonists.

All temporary characters should be approved by a senior gamemaster prior to creation.

4.2 Narrative Authority

Story Team characters exist to facilitate roleplay, not dominate it.

Narrative characters should not rely upon out-of-character authority, special protections, hidden information, or arbitrary powers to achieve their goals.

Story Team characters are not invincible and should be subject to reasonable consequences resulting from player actions.

When portraying antagonists, leaders, or influential figures, Story Team members should focus on creating opportunities for roleplay rather than forcing specific outcomes.

Narrative characters should be treated as tools for storytelling, not as personal characters or recurring protagonists.

Many Story Team characters exist solely to lose and empower players.

5. Event Management

5.1 Preparation

Before running an event:

  • Objectives should be documented.
  • Potential outcomes should be considered.
  • Risks should be identified.
  • Required staff approvals should be obtained.

Preparation should provide flexibility rather than rigid scripting.

5.2 Improvisation

Players will frequently take unexpected actions.

Story Team members are expected to adapt rather than force events back onto a predetermined path.

Player creativity should be rewarded whenever reasonably possible.

5.3 Escalation

Major developments including:

  • Hold leadership changes
  • Large-scale wars
  • Faction collapses
  • Significant lore changes
  • Server-wide crises

Should be discussed with the Story Team's Head of Staff before implementation.

6. Lore Stewardship

6.1 Consistency

Story Team members are responsible for maintaining consistency with established server lore.

New stories should build upon existing narratives whenever possible.

Lore additions should enhance the setting rather than contradict it.

6.2 Adaptation

Lore exists to support roleplay.

When necessary, the Story Team may adapt or expand upon existing lore to improve gameplay and storytelling, subject to approval.

7. Conflicts of Interest

Story Team members must disclose conflicts of interest where appropriate.

If a storyline directly impacts:

  • Their group or groups
  • Their personal character
  • Their close friends
  • Their personal projects

Typically this will not impact their ability to perform a role within the project, but extra care should be taken to ensure their actions are impartial. If the team member wishes to step away from said project or event, they may do so without judgement.

8. Story Team Misconduct

The following may result in disciplinary action:

  • Abuse of narrative authority
  • Favoritism
  • Metagaming using Story Team knowledge
  • Leaking confidential information
  • Railroading events
  • Intentionally denying player agency
  • Creating stories for personal gain
  • Ignoring Story Team procedures

Disciplinary action may include:

  • Warning
  • Removal from specific projects
  • Demotion
  • Removal from Story Team

Depending on severity.

9. Golden Rule

The purpose of the Story Team is not to tell its own story.

The purpose of the Story Team is to create opportunities for players to tell theirs.

A successful Story Team member leaves players feeling that their actions mattered, their choices had consequences, and their roleplay helped shape the world.
